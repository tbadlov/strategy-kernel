# CTO Impact Dimensions

## Overview

A CTO at a Series C/D or IPO-track company operates across four primary functions and several
cross-cutting executive responsibilities. This reference maps each dimension to the types of
impact that resonate with boards, investors, and executive hiring committees.

The interview workflow should systematically extract achievements from EACH dimension, starting
with the company mission and working outward. Most CTOs under-report 2-3 dimensions because
they default to the function where they spend the most time.

---

## Company Mission Alignment (Extract First)

Before probing individual functions, establish the company context. Every achievement gains
power when framed as advancing the company's strategic mission.

**What to extract:**
- Company mission and vision (in the CTO's own words, not the website copy)
- Company stage when the CTO joined vs. current stage
- Top 3 company strategic priorities during the CTO's tenure
- How the technology organization's mandate connected to those priorities
- Key inflection points (fundraising rounds, market pivots, M&A, IPO preparation)

**Why this matters:** A board member reading the resume asks: "Did this person understand
what the business needed and deploy technology to deliver it?" The company context is the
lens through which every bullet is evaluated.

## The Four Functions

### 1. Product Leadership

**What boards and investors assess:**
- Can this CTO translate market needs into technology-enabled products?
- Did product decisions drive revenue growth and market differentiation?
- Is there evidence of product-market fit being achieved or extended?

**Impact categories to extract:**

| Category | Probing questions | Example metrics |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Product-market fit | "What product capabilities did you build that customers couldn't get elsewhere?" | Win rate change, competitive displacement, NPS/CSAT |
| Revenue-generating features | "Which features or products directly generated revenue?" | ARR attributable to feature, deal size increase, conversion rate |
| Market expansion | "Did your product work open new markets, segments, or geographies?" | New market revenue, geographic expansion, segment penetration |
| AI/ML as product | "How did you use AI/ML to create product differentiation?" | Model accuracy → business outcome, automation rate, customer value add |
| Platform strategy | "Did you build platform capabilities that others build on?" | API adoption, developer ecosystem, partner integrations, GMV |

### 2. Engineering Leadership

**What boards and investors assess:**
- Can this CTO build and scale a high-performing engineering organization?
- Is there engineering discipline (process, quality, velocity)?
- Can the org execute at the pace the business requires?

**Impact categories to extract:**

| Category | Probing questions | Example metrics |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Org building | "How large was the team when you started vs. now? How did you design the org?" | Team size growth, org structure, leadership layers |
| Hiring & talent | "What was your hiring strategy? How did you attract senior talent?" | Hiring velocity, offer acceptance rate, senior hire caliber |
| Engineering velocity | "How did you measure and improve engineering throughput?" | DORA metrics (deploy freq, lead time, MTTR, change failure rate) |
| Process maturity | "What engineering processes did you establish?" | Sprint cadence, code review practices, RFC/ADR process |
| Culture | "What engineering culture did you build? How did you handle disagreements?" | Retention rate, eNPS, promotion-from-within rate |
| Technical debt strategy | "How did you balance velocity with quality? What was your tech debt approach?" | Tech debt ratio, planned vs. unplanned work ratio |

### 3. Data Leadership

**What boards and investors assess:**
- Is data treated as a strategic asset?
- Can the organization make data-driven decisions?
- Is there a foundation for AI/ML capabilities?

**Impact categories to extract:**

| Category | Probing questions | Example metrics |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Data platform | "What data infrastructure did you build?" | Data freshness, query performance, warehouse cost efficiency |
| Business intelligence | "How did you enable data-driven decision making?" | Decision latency, self-serve analytics adoption, dashboard usage |
| Data quality | "How did you ensure data reliability?" | Data quality score, SLA compliance, incidents from bad data |
| AI/ML infrastructure | "What ML platform capabilities did you establish?" | Model deployment time, experiment velocity, model monitoring |
| Data governance | "How did you handle data privacy, compliance, and access control?" | GDPR/CCPA compliance, access audit pass rate, PII handling |
| Analytics as revenue | "Did data capabilities become a product feature or revenue driver?" | Data-product revenue, customer value from analytics features |

### 4. Infrastructure Leadership

**What boards and investors assess:**
- Will the platform scale with the business?
- Is the system reliable and secure?
- Is cloud spend managed responsibly?

**Impact categories to extract:**

| Category | Probing questions | Example metrics |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Cloud architecture | "What are the key architectural decisions you made?" | Multi-region, microservices, event-driven, serverless |
| Reliability | "What uptime/SLA do you achieve? How did you get there?" | Uptime %, MTTR, incident frequency, SLA compliance |
| Security & compliance | "What certifications did you achieve? How did you approach security?" | SOC2, PCI-DSS, ISO27001, pentest frequency, vulnerability SLA |
| Cost optimization | "How did you manage cloud spend as you scaled?" | Cost per transaction, cloud spend as % of revenue, unit economics |
| Scalability | "What scale does your platform handle? How did you prove it can grow?" | Transactions/sec, concurrent users, data volume, load test results |
| DevOps/SRE | "What deployment and operations model did you establish?" | Deploy frequency, rollback rate, automated test coverage |

## Cross-Cutting Executive Dimensions

These cut across all four functions and are often the most differentiating at the CTO level.

### Board & Investor Communication
- Presented technology strategy to the board
- Participated in fundraising (investor DD, pitch support)
- Provided technology input on M&A evaluation
- Created investor-grade technology narratives

### Cross-Functional Leadership
- Aligned product, engineering, data, and infrastructure toward shared goals
- Partnered with CEO on company strategy
- Collaborated with CFO on technology budgeting and unit economics
- Worked with Sales/CS on technical sales support and customer success

### M&A & Technical Integration
- Evaluated acquisition targets for technical compatibility
- Led post-acquisition technology integration
- Migrated acquired company's platform/data
- Retained and integrated acquired engineering teams

### IPO / Scale Readiness
- Prepared technology organization for IPO-level scrutiny
- Established controls for SOX compliance
- Built audit trails and disclosure-ready systems
- Achieved enterprise-grade security and reliability benchmarks

---

## Extraction Priority

When time is limited, prioritize extraction in this order:

1. **Company mission alignment** — without this, bullets lack strategic framing
2. **Business outcomes across all four functions** — breadth demonstrates executive scope
3. **Cross-cutting executive dimensions** — these differentiate CTO from VP Engineering
4. **Depth on 1-2 functions** — the user's strongest areas, with the richest metrics
